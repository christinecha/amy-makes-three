SYNOPSIS

AMY MAKES THREE

Writer/Director/Producer: Josh Sternfeld
Producers: Michael Goodin, Scott Kluge
Monolith Pictures, Tremendous Entertainment




Carla and Greg Forrest have the perfect married life. Attractive, well-off, and loving in their relationship, they are happily preparing for the birth of their first child. However, tragedy strikes - they lose their baby Amy, after she's born prematurely. Although both are shocked by the death, Greg tries to move forward with their lives as a couple. But Carla cannot, and in her grief welcomes a delusion that Amy is still alive in their house.


Unfortunately, Carla is right about that.


Amy Forrest, now 9 years old, is most definitely in the house - a demanding, troubled young ghost that threatens to haunt Carla and Greg until they join her on the other side.


In the spirits of The Shining, The Others and Black Swan - AMY MAKES THREE is a taut, electrifying thriller; that uses both psychological and supernatural dimensions to tell a powerful story about loss, spirit, and catharsis.

TEAM



Josh Sternfeld

Josh Sternfeld studied filmmaking at NYU Graduate Film School, where he wrote and directed the award-winning short film Balloons, Streamers. The film screened at the 1997 New York Film Festival, 1998 Sundance Film Festival, and was distributed by the Sundance Channel and Canal +, where it broadcast from 1999-2002.


After completing his degree, Josh worked as an editor for Steeplechase Films on the Emmy-nominated PBS series New York: A Documentary Film. Concurrent with the series,Josh wrote his first feature screenplay Winter Solstice. He developed the script at the 2001 Sundance Screenwriters Lab, and directed the film in 2003; starring Anthony LaPaglia, Allison Janney, Michelle Monaghan and Mark Webber. The film premiered at the Tribeca Film Festival and was acquired by Paramount Classics, who released it internationally in April 2005.


In 2008 Josh wrote and directed Meskada, a police drama set in upstate New York. The film stars Kellan Lutz, Rachel Nichols and Norman Reedus; it premiered at the 2010 Tribeca Film Festival. Inferno Entertainment handled foreign sales, Red Flag Releasing acquired North American theatrical rights, and Time Warner Home Entertainment released it in December 2010. Additional information at meskada.com.

This year, Josh wrote and directed his first thriller, Amy Makes Three, starring Torrey deVitto. The film is currently in post-production and looking at a festival premiere in early 2016.


In addition to his professional work, Josh has taught screenwriting and film direction at New York University/Tisch School of the Arts. He lives with his wife Andrea and daughter Margaux in New York City.
